Filipino Cuisine

Experience the rich flavors and traditions of the Philippines

About Filipino Food

Filipino cuisine is a unique blend of indigenous flavors and foreign influences. It is known for balancing sweet, sour, and savory tastes, often served in hearty, colorful meals. From street food favorites to festive dishes, Filipino food is all about flavor, family, and celebration.

Adobo

A savory-sour dish made with chicken or pork, marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Often considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines.

Sinigang

A sour soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and fresh vegetables, usually served with pork, shrimp, or fish. Perfectly tangy and comforting.

Lechon

Roasted whole pig with crispy skin, typically served during celebrations and fiestas. A symbol of Filipino festivity and indulgence.
